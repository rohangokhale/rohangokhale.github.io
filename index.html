About Me

My first undergraduate degree was in physics, and upon graduating I accepted a job teaching high school physics. I loved the profession even more than I thought I would. Interacting with students each day and sharing the beauty of physics was incredibly enjoyable, as well as rewarding. During my summers off, I took up programming as a hobby, which started as coding simple programs to verify math and physics concepts 'experimentally' rather than theoretically. That quickly turned into building small projects using an Arduino.

As I continued teaching, I found myself missing the feeling of being stimulated by problem solving and creating things. To scratch that itch, I started taking computer science classes with Oregon State University and became incredibly interested in the world of CS. Eventually I decided to pursue a degree in CS full time with the goal of becoming a software engineer. I learned a great deal at OSU and during several side projects that I've created, and as a result am more excited than ever to pursue a career in software engineering.

Twitter Sentiment Analysis and Visualization

Implemented a naive bayes sentiment analysis algorithm trained with a dataset from the University of Michigan. Developed a program that uses the Twitter stream API to collect ~300,000 tweets a day relating to up-to-the-hour trending topics, which are then scored for sentiment. The results are loaded into a state map visualization using D3.js.
